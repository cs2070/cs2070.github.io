Hi, my name is Christine.
I am a UX/UI designer, researcher, marketer, educator, and life-long learner. I enjoy investigating the problems and finding the solutions driven by the data.

I am especially passionate about designing effective interaction systems for new technologies that can benefit people’s lives.




My Story

Ever since majored in Art History at the undergraduate, I’ve deeply interested in learning about visual communication tools across cultures. Images, colors, and signs are major communication tools in the interaction among humans from different cultures, moreover with every technological artifact surrounding us.

To expand and explore these interests, I attended graduate schools in Seoul and New York and involved in various projects and published several papers. I researched how toy robots can become an effective medium in teaching social behavior for children with autism, and participated in the International Development Project on developing effective mobile games to teach English to students in Mongolia.

Most recently, as a marketing manager for a robotic company, I’ve played major role in localizing apps for robot products and designing local voice interfaces for robot vacuums. .
